This project is a complete end-to-end data analysis workflow built entirely with SQL, designed to replicate real industry-level data analytics tasks using the Zepto product dataset. The aim was to transform raw, unstructured data into meaningful business insights without relying on Python, Excel, or BI toolsâ€”showcasing pure SQL capability.
Throughout the project, I worked with thousands of product records containing pricing details, discounts, category information, stock levels, and weights. I began with data profiling and validation, ensuring the dataset was clean and consistent. This included identifying null values, removing incorrect entries like zero-priced products, and converting paise into rupees for accurate financial calculations.

Once cleaned, the data was analyzed to answer real business questions such as:
-Which categories generate the highest potential revenue?
-What products are out of stock despite having a high MRP?
-How many products fall under low, medium, or bulk weight categories?
-Are there anomalies like incorrect pricing or missing values?
-How is the inventory distributed across categories and stock statuses?

Using grouping, filtering, aggregations, and CASE statements, I extracted insights related to revenue forecasting, category performance, customer demand patterns, and inventory health. These insights reflect common tasks performed by analysts in e-commerce and retail companies.

Overall, this project demonstrates strong SQL expertise, attention to detail, and data-driven thinking. It highlights my ability to clean, process, and analyze real-world data solely through SQL queries and convert raw tables into actionable business intelligence.
